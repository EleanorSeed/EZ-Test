Emily Zurowski
Manchester-based Sound Designer & Composer

Emily is a Scottish composer, sound designer, and performer based in Manchester. She has worked as a session musician with artists such as The Orielles and WH Lung (keys, synth, vocals), touring internationally and performing at festivals like SXSW and Green Man Festival. More recently, Emily supported US artist Julie Byrne at the Glasshouse in Gateshead.

In the last year, she has co-created several immersive installations as part of a Manchester-based collective and composed original soundtracks for two documentaries by award-winning documentary maker Maire Tracey. Emily is passionate about composition and currently releases music under the artist name MODEMA - her debut track Running Back received praise from Clash Magazine and BBC Radio 6 Music. Notably, Emily's collaborative track with Ecuadorian composer Joaquin Cornejo (Laughter of the Sea), released under the moniker Mola Mola, has accumulated over 9 million streams to date.

Emily also hosted the radio show Hypnagogia on Reform Radio for a number of years and has produced mixes for Voices Radio and NTS Radio. She works part-time at music-led bar and venue Renae, where she sells records for Patrick Ryder's Future Volumes and takes on the ocassional DJ set. Emily's current interests lie in community-focused projects and using the arts as a vehicle for social change.

Current Projects/Upcoming Events

Secret Lake Stories [podcast/soundscape]: Read more here

'Did you know there’s a hidden lake in Levenshulme? Tucked just off the Fallowfield Loop, the Secret Lake was once an industrial reservoir. Over the years, it’s been left to rewild and take on a life of its own. Today, it’s a quiet, green space full of trees, water birds - a place that means a lot to many local people. Now, we want to gather the voices of the people who know it best. We’re working with sound artist Emily Zurowski to create a new audio piece about the Secret Lake and we want to hear your memories, reflections and stories about the lake.' [Levenshulme Old Library]
