Emily Zurowski
Manchester-based Sound Designer & Composer

Emily is a Scottish composer, sound designer, and performer based in Manchester. She has worked as a session musician with artists such as The Orielles (keys, synth, vocals), who she toured with nationally and internationally for a number of years, performing at festivals like SXSW and Green Man Festival. She has also toured across the UK with WH Lung and performed with them most recently at Green Man Festival in August 2025. As a solo artist, Emily supported established US singer-songwriter Julie Byrne at the Glasshouse in Gateshead. She also performs as part of experimental electronic duo Becoming Air, who debuted at NightGarden Festival in 2025.

In the last year, Emily has co-created several immersive installations as part of a Manchester-based collective and composed original soundtracks for two documentaries by award-winning documentary maker Maire Tracey. She is passionate about composition and currently releases music under the artist name MODEMA - her debut track Running Back received praise from Clash Magazine and BBC Radio 6 Music. Notably, Emily's collaborative track with Ecuadorian composer Joaquin Cornejo has accumulated over 9 million streams to date.

Emily also hosted the radio show Hypnagogia on Reform Radio for a number of years and has produced mixes for Voices Radio and NTS Radio. Her current interests lie in community-focused projects and using the arts as a vehicle for social change. Right now, she is creating a soundscape and podcast about developments on greenspace with residents of Levenshulme, Manchester.

Take a look at some examples of her work below and find her contact details and a full CV at the bottom of the page.
